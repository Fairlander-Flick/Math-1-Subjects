# AI Math Lecture Checklist

### 1. Logic & Quantifiers

- [ ] **Statements & Connectors:** $P \land Q$, $P \lor Q$, $\neg P$, $P \implies Q$, $P \iff Q$.
    
- [ ] **Truth Tables:** Proving Tautologies and Contradictions.
    
- [ ] **Logical Laws:** De Morgan's, Distributive, and Commutative properties.
    
- [ ] **Quantifiers:** Universal ($\forall$), Existential ($\exists$), and Unique ($\exists!$).
    
- [ ] **Conditionals:** Necessary and Sufficient conditions.
    

### 2. Set Theory & Functions

- [ ] **Set Definitions:** Empty set $\emptyset$ and Proper subsets $A \subset B$.
    
- [ ] **Power Sets:** Calculating $\mathcal{P}(S)$ and its cardinality $2^n$.
    
- [ ] **Set Operations:** Union $\cup$, Intersection $\cap$, Difference $\setminus$, and Symmetric Difference $\Delta$.
    
- [ ] **Cartesian Product:** $A \times B = \{(a, b) : a \in A, b \in B\}$.
    
- [ ] **Function Mapping:** Domain, Codomain, Range, and Pre-image $f^{-1}(B)$.
    
- [ ] **Function Types:** Injective (one-to-one), Surjective (onto), and Bijective.
    
- [ ] **Composition:** $(f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))$.
    

### 3. Number Systems & Complex Numbers

- [ ] **Induction Principle:** Proving $P(n)$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$.
    
- [ ] **Elementary Functions:** Absolute value $|x|$, Triangle Inequality $|a+b| \leq |a| + |b|$, Exponentials, and Logarithms.
    
- [ ] **Complex Cartesian Form:** $z = x + iy$, Conjugation $\bar{z}$, and the Complex Plane.
    
- [ ] **Polar & Exponential Forms:** $z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta) = re^{i\theta}$.
    
- [ ] **Roots of Complex Numbers:** Solving $z^n = a$ and graphical symmetry (Regular Polygons).
    

### 4. Polynomials

- [ ] **Polynomial Algebra:** Addition, Multiplication, and Division with Remainder.
    
- [ ] **Root Finding:** Horner's Scheme and the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.
    
- [ ] **Multiplicity:** Identifying single, double, and $n$-fold roots.
    

### 5. Vector Spaces & Linear Maps

- [ ] **Vector Subspaces:** Testing for closure under addition and scalar multiplication.
    
- [ ] **Linear Algebra:** Linear Combinations, Span, and Linear Independence.
    
- [ ] **Bases:** Basis existence, Dimension $\dim(V)$, and Basis Completion.
    
- [ ] **Linear Functions:** Matrix representation $M_B^C(f)$.
    
- [ ] **Fundamental Spaces:** Kernel $\text{Ker}(f)$, Range $\text{Im}(f)$, and Rank-Nullity Theorem.
    

### 6. Linear Systems & Gaussian Elimination

- [ ] **System Types:** Homogeneous ($Ax = 0$) and Inhomogeneous ($Ax = b$).
    
- [ ] **Gaussian Elimination:** Row operations, Pivoting, and Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF).
    
- [ ] **Matrix Algebra:** Matrix-matrix products and Permutation Matrices.
    

### 7. Affine Geometry

- [ ] **Sums of Subspaces:** Direct Sums $U \oplus V$ and dimension calculations.
    
- [ ] **Affine Subspaces:** Parametric representation $P + U$ and intersections of affine spaces.
    

### 8. Norms & Inner Product Spaces

- [ ] **Norms & Metrics:** Euclidean norm $\|x\|_2$, $p$-norms $\|x\|_p$, and Infinity norm $\|x\|_\infty$.
    
- [ ] **Inner Products:** Definitions for Real and Complex spaces, and Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality.
    
- [ ] **Orthogonality:** Orthogonal complements $U^\perp$, Orthogonal Projections, and Orthonormal Bases (ONB).
    
- [ ] **Gram-Schmidt:** Algorithm for converting a basis into an ONB.
    

### 9. Determinants & Least Squares

- [ ] **Determinants:** Laplace Expansion and the Vector Cross Product $u \times v$.
    
- [ ] **Matrix Adjoints:** Conjugate Transpose $A^*$, Symmetric, and Hermitian matrices.
    
- [ ] **Least Squares:** Solving Normal Equations $A^*Ax = A^*b$ to minimize $\|Ax - b\|_2$.
    

### 10. Eigenvalues & Matrix Theory

- [ ] **Eigen-Analysis:** Eigenvalues $\lambda$, Eigenvectors $v$, and Eigenspaces $E_\lambda$.
    
- [ ] **Multiplicities:** Algebraic multiplicity (root of characteristic polynomial) vs. Geometric multiplicity ($\dim(E_\lambda)$).
    
- [ ] **Transformations:** Similarity, Diagonalization, and the Spectral Theorem.
    
- [ ] **Definiteness:** Positive Definite (PD) and Positive Semidefinite (PSD) matrices.
